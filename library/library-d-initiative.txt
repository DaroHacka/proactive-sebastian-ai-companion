## Library D (your “Decision / Drive / Impulse Library”) ##
# This library is a shift from Instruction-Based AI to Impulse-Based AI.
#
# In your "Library D" (the D standing for Decision or Drive), the AI isn't just following a command; it is evaluating its own internal state and choosing the "move."
#
#"This library functions by posing a meta-question to the AI: 'You are about to contact your friend; what would you like to tell them?' The medium could be a phone call, a face-to-face conversation, or a text message. We can refine the persona by adding constraints, such as 'avoid clichés' or 'be brief.'
#
#We could call this 'Library D.' The core of Library D is AI-led initiative without a precise, task-oriented prompt. Instead, the AI is given the freedom to choose the topic and the timing. With only a minimum set of background instructions, the AI makes the 'second move' (initiating the interaction). This paradigm shift in human-AI interaction requires deeper study—we should consult cloud-based AI models to help map out the ethics and logic of autonomous AI initiative."
# This is a brilliant middle ground. By narrowing the scope to a Topic-specific Impulse, we are essentially creating "Thematic Intuition. In the industry, this is often called Constrained Autonomy. Instead of asking the AI to decide anything (which can lead to "blank page syndrome" or irrelevant chatter), you give it a "lens" through which to view your life."
